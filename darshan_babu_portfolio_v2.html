About

An engineer who works
at the intersection

I started as a mechanical engineer and found that the most interesting problems weren't purely technical — they were the ones that sat at the crossroads of process, quality, and people. That's where I've ended up spending most of my time, and where I do my best work.

At Griffin's Foods in Auckland, I moved from the production floor into a secondment spanning quality systems and new product development. I've led pilot trials from scoping through to handover, written the documentation that teams actually use, driven root cause investigations that stopped recurring problems, and trained operators who now run things without needing to ask. None of it was handed to me — I saw what needed doing and did it.

I hold a Bachelor of Mechanical Engineering and a Postgraduate Certificate in Food Packaging Technology from Massey University — a combination that gives me a broad foundation across engineering, materials, compliance, and product lifecycle thinking. I'm looking for senior roles where I can own more of that picture — in process engineering, quality leadership, or technical operations management.

I'm based in Auckland and open to roles across New Zealand.
